I was a part of this meaningful thesis to design Inclusive Design guidelines in the self-driving bus for people who have cognitive impairment.

PROBLEM

How the self-driving bus can be more inclusive in 6 months

Individuals with disabilities often face challenges in transportation, Currently, there is no guarantee that autonomous vehicles will be accessible to the broader disability community when they are deployed

 The main goals of this thesis are:
  1. What are the basic requirements and information needs that people with cognitive disabilities have when traveling with public transportation?
  2. What are inclusive design challenges and opportunities for an Autonomous Vehicle service?

Understanding user journeys

At the beginning of the project, We needed more data to see the bigger picture. We used various types of user research methods to understand the AV context, how our target user commutes a bus and their experiences. I planned and executed user research from interviewing, surveys to analyzing data.

Photo by: Xinshu Li

Key insights

We found that users who have cognitive impairments have problem related with time, emotion and memory.

  • User breaks down easily when an unexpected situation happens and ruin his/her routine
  • User needs more time to remember the route(e.g more than 2 -3 times) and use symbols to check where they are in the process
  • Some of the people with autism and down syndrome may have panic and social anxiety in a crowded place
  • People with mild disorder lack the ability of immediate problem-solving
  • Person with emotional impairments may need to ask several times for clarification and direction
  • The behavior of a bus driver has a significant impact on passengers both physically and mentally

Emphasize target users

We utilized the research data to create a persona. Applying an Inclusive design concept, our personas represented a wide range of users such as general users, people with autism, down syndrome, and caregivers. Personas with disabilities help us to have a more understanding of our target user.

DEFINE

What will happen when there is no bus driver

As I mentioned, the bus driver is an important part of the journey. But if the target user had to commute an AV bus without the driver what will happen. The user journey map was used to discover the pain points and design opportunities during the journey.

Key pain points and needs

  • There is no board or announcement on the AV bus to show where the current position is
  • User feel stress when he always needs to check where he is by looking outside the bus and check for the landmark
  • User has a hard time finding a bus stop platform
  • User feel panic and stress when the bus is late without knowing a reason

Design sprint

We chose the design sprint to fasten our design process and increase a chance to explore more design opportunities and solutions. We did 3 weeks of design sprints to ensure we explore every aspect of the design opportunities.

Monday - Map

Monday is the day tofind/pivot important problems/desgin to focus on as well as conclude who is the target user for the current sprint and how/where to find them. In the next sprint, Monday is also used for analyzing the data from the test from the previous Friday. Furthermore, we chose to post our surveys on the Reddit community (e.g. down syndrome and autism) in every sprint.

Tuesday - Sketch

After having a focused problem/design, ideation and sketching for solutions were conducted on Tuesday with various methods. For example, Cazy's 8, Brainstorming, etc.

Wednesday - Choose

From many sketches and ideas, feasible ones were selected by voting and turned into testable hypotheses on Wednesday. However, two ideas needed to be selected in each hypothesis to test as A/B testing.

Thursday - Prototype

Prototypes were built on Thursday. They had to be a Hi-Fi prototype because it was difficult for the target user to understand the unorganized sketch/ low-fi prototype.

Friday - A/B Testing and Surveys

The surveys were created as a storyboard. It contained the scenario and choices for the user to follow and choose.

DELIVER

Initail solutions

After validating every idea, we chose the highest vote idea for each stage of the transportation. We finalized and re-designed based on the feedback and included some good points from other designs. The footprint to the bus stop, bus stop sign, AI interactive signage with a script and information on board.

USABILITY TESTING

Physical Testing

To test the first design approach of navigation footprint and navigation signage, a test was conducted at the bus platform Regnbågsgatan in Gothenburg. Four participants were recruited for the test. From the user research in the previous phase, most users found that the AV bus station was hard to spot, thus in this test participants were given the task to the AV bus platform from the normal bus platform. This test also aimed to simulate the real scenario that users transiting buses between bus platforms.

Digital testing

Participants were first introduced to the scenarios so they can test with a target user's perspective in mind. Afterward, participants were asked to perform the Think-aloud with whatever they were looking at, thinking, doing, and feeling while searching the route to their destination. A heuristic evaluation of the design’s visibility, assistance, and efficiency was done after people finish their tasks.

RESULT

7 Inclusive design guidelines

Based on previous literature studies and design processes, we proposed 7 inclusive design guidelines of information needs in AV service.

REFLECTIONS
Diverse characteristics of people with cognitive impairment

I found out that users can have different symptoms even though they have the same disorder. When working with this type of user, it is hard to balance how much assistance from the design is suitable. So, I think we should avoid making users feel uncomfortable and creating a design that underestimates their ability.

It is important to consider the meaning behind the words when talking about a specific group of people. Using wrong language can make people feel excluded.

User research and test

We had difficulties conducting real-life testing with people with cognitive disabilities or contacting experts. We managed to learn about our target through experts and literature. However, I think the result might be different if we have a chance to conduct real-life observations and interviews with people who have cognitive impairment.

Trust and Safety

During the project, many users expressed concern about it. In qualitative research, there is a scenario of the AV bus encountering a sudden error, and participants were asked for their opinions. Most participants trust AI, but they expected the AV bus can provide human support when severe accidents happen. I think a combination between AI and human service could gain more trust in the system.
