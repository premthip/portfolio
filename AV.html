Problem

Access to public transportation is a key need for people. However, individuals with disabilities often face challenges in transportation, particularly those with severe cognitive impairments. Currently, there is no guarantee that autonomous vehicles will be accessible to the broader disability community when they are deployed

Goal

The project aims to adopt an inclusive design approach and accessibility guidline to improve the Autonomous Vehicle (AV) shuttle bus with a focus on commuters and bus riders who have cognitive impairment.

Research question

What challenges and opportunities do AI and sensors bring for passengers in an Autonomous Vehicle service when there is no bus driver?

Challenges

Under the covid restriction, we cannot study an interaction between the Autonomous Vehicle shuttle bus and passenger who has cognitive impairments. We can only rely on points of view from experts. More than 20 interview invitations have been sent out to experts(e.g caregiver) or organizations which work with people with cognitive disabilities. However, only 3 experts replied back.

Process

This thesis project follows the Double Diamond process. In this framework, the design process is divided into four distinct phases: Discover, Define, Develop and Deliver, which maps the divergent and convergent stages of the design process.

Discover

User Research

User research was conducted to understand functions of the current AV bus service, responsibilities of the AV and normal bus driver, and users' difficulties and challenges during a journey.

Interview with bus drivers and general passengers

We came to understand that the bus can monitor the situations and provide appropriate operation itself.

Interviewing a bus driver, we found out which function could be replace by AI in the future. We also interviewed general passengers for their experience on the AV bus to support a diversity perspective.

Interview with experts

Due to the epidemic restiction, we didn't have a chance to interview people with cognitive disabilities in a real context. We researched literatures related to cognitive impairment and interviewed experts to understand the target users’ behavior and difficulties when commuting with public transportation.

Survey

Surveys with questionaires were sent out to understand the interaction and conversation between target user and a bus driver. For example,what kind of information do you ask a bus driver? Why do you need a bus driver?

Data Clustering

The data was categorized with Affinity diagram to understand which points need to be focused and used as a main key point in Personas.

Key insights of user characteristic during the journey
  • User breaks down easily when an unexpected situation happens and ruin his/her routine.
  • User needs more time to remember the route(e.g more than 2 -3 times) and use the symbole to check where they are in the process.
  • Some of the people with autism and down syndrome might have panic and social anxiety.
  • People with mild disorder lacke the ability of immediate problem-solving.
Key insights of interaction between user and bus driver
  • Ask for direction or anything realated to the bus condition.
  • Ask for assistant when unexpected events happens.
  • Ask for position of nearby places.
  • Ask to comfirm the user/bus' current position.
  • A bus driver behavior and emtional have a significantly effect on passengers experience.

We utilized the research data to create persona. Personas representes a general user, people with autism, down syndrome and caregiver. They were used for creating a user journey map to discover and define the problems, motivation, emotion and requirements of target users in the AV bus context.

Define

What is the problem when target users use the AV bus service? (without the driver)

The user journey map was used to discover the painpoint and opportunities during the journey. There are two main journey maps that we look in the deeper level. The post-it at the buttom of the map, represented the information needs at each stage of the transportation.

Key insights of information needs
  • How long do I need to wait for the AV bus?
  • Where am I right now(on the bus)?.
  • Can I use the same ticket as a normal bus?
  • Why is the bus is so late?.
  • Where is the bus stop?.
  • How can I go to my destination?.
Key insights of user painpoint
  • There is no board or announcement on the AV bus to show where the current position is.
  • User feel stress when he needs to check where he is by looking outside and check for the landmark.
  • User have a hard time finding a bus stop platform.
  • User feel panic and stress when the bus is late.

After that we categorized problems into what we can solve wiht the desgin and what we cannot due to complexities of syndrome. Then we used the method "how might we" to generate initial solutions for problems.

How might we
  • make the warning sound less annoying?
  • make the bus stop visible and distinguishable?
  • decrease the stress and anxiety in the bus?
  • inform users where is the bus right now?
  • make the instruction/information of the AV bus simple to remember?
  • help users get off the bus at the correct destination?
  • help users navigate to the correct direction after the bus?

Develop

Google Design Sprint is a five-days process of exploring good design solutions through ideation, prototyping, and evaluation. In the development stage, the three-weeks design sprint was planned to filter ideas, narrow down the scope, and test with target users

Monday - Map

Monday is the day to find/pivot important problems/desgin to focus on as well as conclude who is the target user for the current sprint and how/where to find them. In next sprint, the monday is also used for analysing the data from the test from previous Friday. Furthermore, we chose to post our surveys on reddit community (e.g. downsyndrome and autism) in every sprint.

Tuesday - Sketch

After having a focused problem/design, an ideation and sketching for solutions were conducted on Tuesday with various methods. For example, Cazy's 8, Brainstorming, etc.

Wednesday - Choose

From many sketches and ideas, feasible ones were selected by voting and turned into testable hypotheses on Wednesday. However, two ideas needed to be selected in each hypotheses in order to test as a A/B testing.

Thursday - Prototype

Prototypes were built on Thursday. They had to be a Hi-Fi prototype because it was difficult for target user to understand the unoriganized sketch/ low-fi prototype.

Friday - Test

The surveys were created as a storyboard. It contained the secenerio and choices for user to follow and choose.

For example, Imagine that you are waiting at the Autonomous Vehicle (AV) bus stop and check the direction you would go. You want to go to Kuggen building. But at the same time you have an issue with an internet connection. Here is the information signage at the bus stop with an AI bot that you can interact with...

Deliver

Initial solutions

After validating every idea, we chose the highest vote idea for each stage of the transportaiton. We finalized and re-designed based on the feedback and included some good points from other designs. Here are our initail solutions: The footprint to the bus stop, bus stop sign, AI interactive signage with a script and information on board.

Usabilty Testing

Participants

From the research, people who are new in the area behave the same way as people with congnitive disabilities during the journey. Hence, we chose users who never been or heard of the AV service before.

Physically testing

To test the first design approach of navigation footprint and navigation signage, a test was conducted at the bus platform Regnbågsgatan in Gothenburg. Four participants were recruited in the test. From the user research in the previous phase, most users found that the AV bus station was hard to spot, thus in this test participants were given the task to the AV bus platform from the normal bus platform. This test also aimed to simulate the real scenario that users transiting bus between bus platforms.

Digitaly testing

Participants were firstly introduced with the scenarios so they can test with a target user's perspective in mind. Afterwards, participants were asked to perform the Think-aloud with whatever they were looking at, thinking, doing, and feeling while searching the route to their destination. A heuristic evaluation of the design’s visibility, assistance, and efficiency was done after people finish their task.

Result

The navigation design for people with congnitive disabilities
Footprint and Arrow signage

The footprint and arrow signage are uncomplicated and easy for user to understand and follow. Furthermore, The footprints were consistently placed on the pavement in the physical test which assisted people to find the destination efficiently. The color distinct from the ground makes it more visible. Users pointed out that they only needed to follow the footprint which they know that they will not lose the way to the bus platform.

Bus stop

When designing information signage for AV service, the name of the AV bus stop is an indispensable factor, the direction and timetable should be displayed repeatedly for passengers to notice, and the instruction of ticket information is crucial for first-time users to remember.

If there is no bus driver providing a service
The Interactive AI map

Every day, there will be users who ask a bus driver for their directions. We replaced the driver by providing all the information on the interactive board. The approach of the AI route planning assistant will give a same information as a bus driver. Inputting the destination, passenger can receive the route information in the form of a script. This can assist any type of passengers to find their direction.

The script can be synced to mobile phones or taken the photo in case people forget the route. During transit process, it is essential to provide the route information both online and offline to fulfill the requirement of passengers with different navigation behavior.

If there is no bus driver on board
Annoucment and information signage on board

The announcement in audible and visual form is the basic requirement of public transportation. Hence, both audible and visual announcements of the bus stops are required. From the surveys, research and testing, users stated that if there is a clear announcement and information on the bus it is enough for their journey.

If there is an unexpected event happend (e.g accident, the bus broken) We found that the calm and humane announcement can reduce stress and assist them to perform the right actions

Conclusion

What challenges and opportunities do AI and sensors bring for passengers in an Autonomous Vehicle service when there is no bus driver?

Opportunities
  • From a business perspective, they can reduce the hiring cost in a transportation
  • AI can provide equal service, with no bias for every user and no emotional involvement
  • Reduce human error in the system
Challenges
  • AI service needs to be develop behavior close to human-like to assist a special individual needs
  • When unexpected situation happens, we need to design how AI handle this situation as same as a bus driver
  • We need to think about how AI can mentally assist passengers who has mentally impairments
  • AI Software needs to be improve to increase usability and trust

Learn & Insights

Lastly, we experienced many unexpected events and obstacles at the beginning of the project. We have learnt valuable lessons from this project and these are what I learnt and reflected.

Choosing target User

We found out that even though the target user have a same disorder but they can have a different syntoms. Thus, we need to be very specific in choosing the user characteristic. Try to narrow it down as much as posssible.

Testing

We had difficulties conducting real-life testing with people with congnitive disabilities or contacting experts. The test data and outcome could be different from the current on. More time should be spent on adapting the framework to specifically fit the target group.

AI's effect

When adopting an AI to create a UX, we need to consider many perspective and limitation. In this project, we only touch the surface of Human AI interaction. This project can be investigate further in the future

Trust & Safety

During this thesis, we receive many feedback related the trust and safety. Some users trust an AI, some not. This issues can be investigate in term of funtionally or culture.
